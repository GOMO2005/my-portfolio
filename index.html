Optical Computers

An Optical Computer uses light instead of electricity for processing, offering faster speeds and improved energy efficiency. It utilizes two different colors of light: one (e.g., red) for high-speed tasks and the other (e.g., blue) for more complex, slower tasks, enabling parallel processing. This design increases computational speed, reduces heat generation, and enhances multitasking capabilities, making it ideal for fields like AI, cryptography, and scientific simulations
